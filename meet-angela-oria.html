    An an undergraduate engineer at the Waterloo Metro Station project, being my first ever work experience. I was tasked to administer, manage and deliver the signage package for the whole station.   • üßóüèª‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è How I was able to move forward

    Due to this initial exposure of the industry and liaising with multiple consultants whom I had never met. It was definite that taking initiative was my only play. First, I asked the services team who had dealt with these consultants for their contacts. After listing the contacts, I had checked each individual reports e.g. BCA for Waterloo, Fire Engineering Report and Acoustic Report and filtered any requirements for signage. Next, I collated all signage that needed to be addressed, as well as, found additional signage that had been missed in the drawings and put these into an excel sheet addressing their ID codes, art, dimension, quantity, compliance requirement and comments from the architects where certain signage were pending confirmation. Afterwards, I organised a time with individual consultants to address what was on the sheet and filtered their relevant ones so I could track the progress and comments accurately by the date on one document. Lastly, I collected all the data, confirmed the changes and delivered the solutions to the architects using Notion on a database table via a teams meeting I had also arranged.
